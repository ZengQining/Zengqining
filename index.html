HDB Residents' Accessibility to Parks and Water Activities Areas

HDB Residents' Accessibility to Parks and Water Activities Areas is a data visualization project that examines the accessibility of green and blue spaces for residents living in Singapore’s public housing estates.

The storymap leverages government open data to map the locations of parks, reservoirs, and designated water activity areas, calculating the walkshed of access within a 500-meter network distance for parks and water activity areas from all HDB flats in Singapore.

By highlighting gaps in accessibility, this project provides insights into how urban planning can enhance recreational equity and support Singapore’s vision of becoming a “City in Nature.”
